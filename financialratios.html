Liquidity Ratios


Measures a company's ability to meet short-term and long-term obligations.


Current Ratio: Ability to pay off short-term liabilities with current assets.
Acid-Test Ratio: Ability to pay off short-term liabilities with quick assets.
Cash Ratio: Ability to pay off short-term liabilities with cash and cash equivalents.
Operating Cash Flow Ratio: The number of times a company can pay off current liabilities with the cash generated in a given period.


Leverage Financial Ratios


Measures the amount of capital that comes from debt.


Debt Ratio: The relative amount of a company's assets that are provided from debt.
Debt To Equity Ratio: Weight of total debt and financial liabilities against shareholders' equity.
Interest Coverage Ratio: How easily a company can pay its interest expenses.
Debt Service Coverage Ratio: How easily a company can pay its debt obligations.


Efficiency Ratios


Measures how well a company is utilizing its assets and resources.


Asset Turnover Ratio: A company's ability to generate sales from assets.
Inventory Turnover Ratio: How many time a company's inventory is sold and replaced over a given period.
Receivables Turnover Ratio: How many times a company can turn receivables into cash over a given period.
Days Sales In Inventory Ratio: The average number of days a company holds on to inventory before selling it to customers.


Profitability Ratios


Measures a company's ability to generate income relative to revenue, balance sheet assets, operating costs, and equity.


Gross Margin Ratio: Compares the gross profit of a company to its net sales.
Operating Margin Ratio: Compares the operating income of a company to its net sales.
Return on Assets Ratio: How efficently a company is using its assets to generate profit.
Return on Equity Ratio: How efficiently a company is using its equity to generate profit.


Market Value Ratios


Evaluates the share price of a company's stock.


Book Value Per Share Ratio: Calculates the per-share value of a company based on the equity available to shareholders.
Dividend Yield Ratio: The amount of dividends attributed to shareholders relative to the market value per share.
Earnings Per Share: The amount of net income earned for each share outstanding.
Price To Earings Ratio: Compares a company's share price to its earnings per share.
